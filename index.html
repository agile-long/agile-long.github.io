Abstract

Long-horizon manipulation requires an agent to compose many sub-tasks while staying robust to compounding error. We present AGiLe, a framework that grounds object affordances and performs bidirectional latent planning: forward rollouts predict reachable states from the current observation, while backward reasoning from the goal constrains the search toward feasible solutions. By aligning these two directions in a shared latent space, AGiLe produces plans that are both goal-consistent and physically grounded.
